Pongal Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup split yellow moong dal
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 8-10 cashew nuts
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash rice and moong dal together, and set aside.
  2. Heat ghee in a pressure cooker or heavy-bottomed pot.
  3. Add cumin seeds, black peppercorns, grated ginger, cashew nuts, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add the washed rice and moong dal to the pot and sauté for a couple of minutes.
  5. Add water and salt, and pressure cook for 3-4 whistles (or cook in a pot until the rice and dal are soft).
  6. Mash the cooked rice and dal mixture and adjust the consistency with water if needed.
  7. Serve hot with sambar, coconut chutney, or ghee.

Serving Suggestions:

Pongal is traditionally served with sambar, coconut chutney, and a dollop of ghee.

Tips:

  • You can add a handful of freshly grated coconut for added flavor.
  • For a spicier version, you can add green chilies during the tempering.
  • To make it healthier, use brown rice instead of white rice.
  • Don't skip the ghee; it adds a rich and aromatic flavor to the dish.
  • If you don't have a pressure cooker, you can cook Pongal in a regular pot; it will just take a bit longer.
